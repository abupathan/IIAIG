Responsible use of contact channels

  • Do not send passwords, financial account numbers, health records or other highly sensitive data through general contact forms.
  • For issues involving legal deadlines, regulatory filings or urgent safety matters, follow your organization’s official escalation procedures rather than relying solely on a website form.
  • If your inquiry involves personal data about others, ensure you are authorized to share it, limit what you send to what is necessary, and respect relevant privacy obligations.
  • In a production site, this guidance should align with your Terms of Use, Privacy Policy, Cookie Notice and AI governance / incident response playbooks.

In production, consider adding CAPTCHA or similar controls to manage spam and abuse, and ensure that backend handling of form submissions (including any AI-assisted routing or summarization) is secure, auditable and documented.

Future-ready AI-assisted communication (template, non-binding)

Over the 2020s and 2030s, professional institutes may use AI-assisted tools to triage, summarize and route inbound communications. This non-binding template section highlights considerations if such tools are used in connection with this contact page in a real deployment.

Human-in-the-loop handling

AI systems may help categorize and route messages, but human reviewers should remain responsible for final interpretation, especially for legal, ethics or incident-related inquiries. Any such use should be clearly disclosed in the Privacy Policy and related notices.

Privacy & logging

If communications are processed by AI tools, logging and retention practices should respect data protection law and AI governance frameworks, with clear limits on who can access logs and for what purposes (for example, quality review, incident analysis).

Transparency to senders

If AI is used for triage or summarization, visitors should be informed in clear language (for example, via the Privacy Policy, a banner or contextual notice) and be able to use alternative channels where AI-assisted handling is not appropriate.

Any live implementation of AI-assisted communication must be designed within your broader AI governance framework, legal obligations and risk appetite, and reflected consistently across this page and your legal notices.
